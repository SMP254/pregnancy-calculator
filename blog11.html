10 First Trimester Must-Haves Every Mom-to-Be Should Know

Support your early pregnancy with comfort, nutrition, and peace of mind.

That positive test just changed your world. But now you're wondering — what do I actually need in the first trimester?

You're not alone! The first 12 weeks bring a mix of excitement, symptoms, and a million questions. We've gathered the most essential first trimester must-haves to help you feel confident, comfortable, and ready.

1. Prenatal Vitamins

A high-quality prenatal vitamin supports your baby’s brain and spine development. Look for ones with folic acid, iron, and DHA.

2. Pregnancy Journal or App

Tracking your symptoms, feelings, and milestones can be empowering. A journal also makes a sweet keepsake later on.

3. Belly Butter or Stretch Mark Cream

Start moisturizing early! Products with shea butter or vitamin E help reduce itching and early stretch marks.

4. Morning Sickness Relief

Whether it's ginger drops, nausea bands, or vitamin B6 tablets — keeping something on hand can save your day.

5. Comfortable Maternity Bra

Your breasts may grow quickly. A soft, stretchy maternity bra gives support without pain or pressure.

6. Hydration Tracker Bottle

Water helps prevent headaches, fatigue, and constipation. A bottle that reminds you to drink = game-changer.

7. Comfy Loungewear or Leggings

Bloating and early bump mean you’ll crave stretchy, breathable clothes more than ever.

8. Mild Pregnancy-Safe Skincare

Some ingredients like retinol are not safe in pregnancy. Switch to gentle cleansers and fragrance-free products.

9. Snack Packs (Protein Bars, Crackers, Nuts)

Keeping small snacks nearby helps with nausea and energy dips throughout the day.

10. Educational Books or Audiobooks

Books like What to Expect When You're Expecting provide helpful guidance as you navigate new changes.

FAQs

What should I buy in my first trimester of pregnancy?

Start with essentials like prenatal vitamins, belly butter, and items for nausea relief. You don't need a lot — just things that support your comfort and health.

When should I start using belly butter?

Start using it in the first trimester to hydrate your skin and prevent early stretch marks.
