Top 10 Early Signs of Pregnancy

Think you might be pregnant? Some early symptoms can appear as soon as a week after conception — even before your missed period.

Here are the most common early signs of pregnancy, and what to look for if you're trying to conceive.

1. 🤢 Nausea or Morning Sickness

This is one of the most well-known symptoms. It can start as early as 2–3 weeks after conception, often without vomiting.

2. 😴 Fatigue

Feeling unusually tired? Rising progesterone levels can make you sleepy all day, even if you’ve had enough rest.

3. 🎯 Missed Period

If your cycle is regular, missing a period is often the first solid clue. But some women have light implantation spotting and mistake it for a period.

4. 💧 Frequent Urination

Hormonal changes increase blood flow to your kidneys and bladder — making you pee more, even before your belly grows.

5. 🐘 Sore or Swollen Breasts

Your breasts may feel fuller, tender, or even tingly — similar to premenstrual symptoms but often more intense.

6. 🎢 Mood Swings

Sudden tears, frustration, or anxiety? Hormonal surges in early pregnancy can affect your emotions quickly.

7. 🍕 Food Cravings or Aversions

You may suddenly crave pickles or feel repulsed by your usual favorite foods. Smell sensitivity also increases.

8. 🌡 Slight Temperature Rise

If you’ve been tracking your basal body temperature (BBT), a persistent rise for 18+ days post-ovulation could mean you're pregnant.

9. 💥 Implantation Bleeding or Cramping

Some women notice light spotting or mild cramping when the fertilized egg attaches to the uterus (usually 6–12 days after ovulation).

10. 😵 Headaches or Dizziness

Low blood pressure and hormonal shifts can trigger headaches or feeling faint, especially in early pregnancy.

🎯 When to Take a Test?

The best time is after your missed period for accurate results. Some sensitive pregnancy tests can detect hCG hormone as early as 6–8 days after ovulation.

Tip: If you’re seeing several of these symptoms, take a test — and confirm with your healthcare provider.

📌 Final Thoughts

Every woman and pregnancy is different. Some feel many symptoms early, while others feel none at all. The only way to confirm is through a test.
